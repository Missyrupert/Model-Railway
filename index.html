History of Model Railways

Model railways first appeared in the Victorian era, captivating generations ever since. The UK led the way with iconic makers like Hornby and Tri-ang, inspiring hobbyists to recreate the golden age of rail in miniature. The rise of clubs, exhibitions, and dedicated magazines from the early 20th century made model railways a beloved pastime for all ages.

In British popular culture, model railways appear in films like "The Railway Children" and TV shows such as "Thomas & Friends." Today, clubs, layouts, and competitions remain at the heart of UK hobby life.

Why Model Railways?

From children to seasoned collectors, model railways attract people from every walk of life. They combine creativity, craftsmanship, and history, offering a relaxing escape and a tangible link to Britain’s transport heritage. Whether building layouts or restoring classic trains, the hobby bridges generations and fosters friendships.
